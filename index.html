Projects

Playing Cards Design

Playing cards redesigned inspired by symbolism art movement. The design has mystic symbols, a feature of symbolism. The logos of the four suits are cut out in the shape of eyes. All these depict the mystic and spiritual elements used in symbolism art. The colour of the cards is blue which represents mystery and fantasy. The layout of the playing cards has been changed as symbolism was about escaping reality and expressing one’s own artistic expression.

Book Cover: Norwegian Wood

‘Norwegian Wood’ is a story of a college student’ s romantic coming-of-age, it takes one to that distant place of a young man ’ s first, hopeless, and heroic love. The final cover carries the same idea- references to forests and woods with the design and the colours used. The cover has a somber effect on the mind- just like the story does.

Book Cover: Remnants Of A Separation

The final cover is a refined version of the ideation. It shows the title in the form of threads, depicting the connection of people to their land. The scissors cutting the word (thread) at the bottom shows the alienation of people when they had to move across the border The colours used are so to show the colourful memories of the past that people still hold close to their hearts before they had to give it all up

Book Cover: The Kite Runner

The Kite Runner tells the story of an unlikely friendship between a wealthy Afghan boy, named Amir, and Hassan, the son of his father's servant — who he later finds out, after Hassan's death, has always been his half-brother. Combining two things that hold a significant symbolic meaning in the book, the final cover was made. The title is written in the form of a kite and a pomegranate has been illustrated behind it.

Expressive Typography

The name which is illustrated in the poster is ‘Gauri’. It is another name for Goddess Parvati. Since the Goddess holds a lotus in her hand, the name was illustrated in the same form. Another version, before finalising the lotus illustration was one of a lion as it is said to be Goddess Parvati’s ‘vahan’, or the creature she rides on.
